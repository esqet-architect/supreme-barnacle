\subsection*{LISA Delay Estimate: GW170817 Constraints}

\subsubsection*{The Problem}
For cosmological GW propagation, even tiny deviations in speed are tightly constrained by multimessenger observations.

GW170817/GRB 170817A gives:
\[
|c_T/c - 1| < 5 \times 10^{-16}
\]

Our estimate $\Delta t \sim 10^3$ s for $D_L = 10^9$ pc corresponds to:
\[
|c_T/c - 1| \sim \frac{\Delta t}{D_L} \sim \frac{10^3}{3\times10^{25}} \sim 3\times10^{-23}
\]

This is within the bound, but the \textit{interpretation} requires careful handling.

\subsubsection*{Revised Wording}
Replace the unconditional claim with:

> "For $\xi \sim 0.1$ and $T_0/\Lambda \sim 1$, the propagation speed is $c_s = 1/\sqrt{1+\xi^2} \approx 0.995$, giving $|c_s/c - 1| \approx 5\times10^{-3}$. This is inconsistent with GW170817 constraints. Therefore, either $\xi$ must be $\ll 0.1$ at GW scales, or the parity-odd propagation is suppressed for $f \sim 100$ Hz relative to cosmological $f$. The latter could occur if the effect is frequency-dependent or if the torsion coupling runs with scale. This whitepaper presents the parametric dependence; observational viability requires $|\xi(f_{\text{GW}})| \ll 0.1$."

This acknowledges the tension rather than ignoring it.
